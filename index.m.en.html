Calculation principle:

Let the two-point coordinate of the input be $$ (\frac{a}{b},\frac{c}{d}), (\frac{e}{f},\frac{g}{h}) $$

Let the analytical expression of the line be $$ y=kx+h $$

Substituting the two point coordinates into the analytical formula, we get $$ \left\{ \begin{aligned} \frac{a}{b}k+h=\frac{c}{d} -- I \\ \frac{e}{f}k+h=\frac{g}{h} -- II \end{aligned} \right. $$

I - II $$ (\frac{a}{b}-\frac{e}{f})k=(\frac{c}{d}-\frac{g}{h}) $$

$$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} k &=(\frac{c}{d}-\frac{g}{h})÷(\frac{a}{b}-\frac{e}{f}) \\ &=\frac{ch-dg}{dh}÷\frac{af-be}{bf} \\ &=\frac{ch-dg}{dh}·\frac{bf}{af-be} \\ &=\frac{bf(ch-dg)}{dh(af-be)} \end{split} \end{equation} $$

Substitute the above equation into I to obtain $$ \frac{a}{b}(\frac{bf(ch-dg)}{dh(af-be)})+h = \frac{c}{d} $$

that is $$ \frac{abf(ch-dg)}{bdh(af-be)}+h = \frac{c}{d} $$

$$ \begin{equation} \begin{split} h &=\frac{c}{d}-\frac{abf(ch-dg)}{bdh(af-be)} \\ &=\frac{bcdh(af-be)-abdf(ch-dg)}{bd^2h(af-be)} \end{split} \end{equation} $$

Therefore, the analytical expression of the line is: $$ y=\frac{bf(ch-dg)}{dh(af-be)}x+\frac{bcdh(af-be)-abdf(ch-dg)}{bd^2h(af-be)} $$
